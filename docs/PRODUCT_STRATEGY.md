# Seller Expansion Studio
## Product Strategy & Demo Narrative

---

## One-Line Pitch

> **Walmart knows what customers want and which sellers can supply it. Seller Expansion Studio turns that signal into a live, optimized listing — in hours, not weeks.**

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Walmart has rich demand intelligence — Customer Favorites, search trends, GMV gap analysis across categories. But this intelligence doesn't automatically translate into seller action. The activation gap costs Walmart and sellers real GMV.

A seller with 300 items today:
- Spends 14+ hours/week on catalog management
- Has 48–76% Listing Quality Score when it should be 88%+
- Is sitting on 10+ high-demand item opportunities they can't act on fast enough
- Takes 2–4 weeks to go from "I should sell this" to "item is live"

**The root cause isn't awareness — it's execution friction.** Sourcing takes days. Listing takes hours. Pricing requires research. Marketing requires strategy. All manual. All fragmented.

---

## What We're NOT Building

We are **not** building:
- A demand discovery platform (Walmart already has assortment growth signals)
- A seller matching algorithm (Walmart's category teams already do this)
- A competitor intelligence tool (out of scope for MVP)

We start after Walmart has already said: *"This seller should be selling this item."*

---

## What We ARE Building

**An agentic activation workspace** that takes Walmart's demand signal + seller fit confirmation and produces a fully launch-ready item — complete with supplier, contract, listing, price, and marketing — in hours.

---

## The Full Product Story

### Phase 1: New Item Activation (The Hero Flow)

**Entry point:** Seller Center Home Page
- Seller logs in and sees a prominent banner: *"Unlock New Opportunities — AI has found 10 items you should be selling"*
- This is the hook. One click begins the journey.

**Step 1: Opportunity Discovery**
- A chat-like interface surfaces 10 items Walmart has identified as high-demand gaps this seller can fill
- Each item shows: demand score, estimated GMV/month, category, Customer Favorite status
- Seller selects which items to pursue (typically 3–5)
- One click: "Activate Selected Items"

**Step 2: Sourcing & Negotiation Agent**
- This is the most differentiated part of the product
- Agent automatically searches Walmart's supplier network + external sources
- Drafts and sends outreach emails to potential suppliers (MOQ, unit cost, lead time, certifications)
- Handles back-and-forth negotiation via email, autonomously
- Returns a ranked shortlist of supplier proposals with pricing, MOQ, lead times, and ratings
- Seller selects preferred supplier per item (this is the seller's decision)

**Step 3: Agent Consent**
- Three agents ask for authorization: Catalog Agent, Pricing Agent, Marketing Agent
- Seller toggles on/off based on their needs
- "Start Agents" — everything runs in the background

**Step 4: Catalog Optimization Agent**
- Creates a Walmart-optimized listing: title (75 chars, keyword-leading), 250-word description, 5 key feature bullets, all required attributes
- Targets Listing Quality Score of 92+
- Uses brand guidelines if provided
- Images flagged/requested if missing

**Step 5: Pricing Agent**
- Benchmarks competitor prices (Amazon, Google Shopping)
- Sets recommended launch price, floor price, Buy Box target
- Configures Repricer strategy from day one
- Checks Flash Deals eligibility

**Step 6: Marketing Agent**
- Creates sponsored search keyword strategy + bid recommendations
- Drafts TikTok/Reels scripts for creator outreach
- Builds launch calendar (when to run Flash Deals, when to request reviews)
- Creator brief for Walmart Creator network

**Step 7: Preview & Publish**
- Seller sees all items with full listing, price, and marketing strategy
- Two actions per item: Preview → Publish
- One click "Publish All" to make everything live

**Result:** Days → Hours. Item is live on Walmart.com with competitive pricing, optimized listing, and a marketing strategy running from day one.

---

### Phase 2: Ongoing Catalog Optimization (The Autopilot)

Once items are live (new or existing catalog), the same three agents run continuously:

**Pricing Agent (always on)**
- Monitors Buy Box and competitor prices
- Repricer adjusts automatically within seller-defined floor/ceiling
- Alerts on pricing opportunities (Flash Deals, price drops that unlock traffic)

**Catalog Agent (weekly runs)**
- Monitors Listing Quality Score for all items
- Accepts SEO Marketing suggestions automatically (low risk)
- Fills in missing attributes, updates keywords based on trend data
- Flags image gaps for seller to address

**Marketing Agent (event-driven)**
- Seasonal campaign adjustments (holiday, back-to-school, etc.)
- Review Accelerator enrollment for new items
- SEM campaign optimization based on performance data

**The Seller Experience:**
- Opens Seller Center once a week
- Sees a Review Tray of 3–8 items that need attention (high-risk changes that agents drafted but didn't auto-apply)
- Approves or rejects in 10 minutes
- Everything else: already done

## The Guardrails System

Sellers define what agents can do automatically:

| Risk Level | Agent Behavior |
|------------|---------------|
| 🟢 Low | Agent acts, logs it, seller sees in weekly digest |
| 🟡 Medium | Agent acts, seller can review/revert within 24 hrs |
| 🔴 High | Agent drafts, seller must approve before publishing |

Default: SEO title/description updates = Low. Full listing rewrites = High. Pricing changes = always reviewed.

This ensures **the seller is always in control** while agents handle the volume.
